## 🎓 Practical Examples

### Example 1: Trapezoid Area
```
Parallel sides: a=8m, b=12m
Height: h=5m
Formula: A = 0.5 × (a + b) × h
Calculation: 0.5 × (8 + 12) × 5 = 0.5 × 20 × 5 = 50
Result: Area = 50 m²
```

### Example 2: Cube Surface Area
```
Side: a=6 cm
Formula: A = 6 × a²
Calculation: 6 × 6² = 6 × 36 = 216
Result: Surface Area = 216 cm²
```

### Example 3: Cone Volume
```
Radius: r=4 cm
Height: h=9 cm
Formula: V = (1/3) × π × r² × h

Result: Volume ≈ 150.80 cm³
```

```
Result: Area ≈ 52.36 cm²
```

## 💡 Tips for Using Geometry Formulas

### 2D Shapes:
- **Area** measures space inside (square units)
- **Perimeter** measures boundary length (linear units)
- For circles, remember π ≈ 3.14159

### 3D Shapes:
- **Volume** measures space inside (cubic units)
- **Surface Area** measures total outer area (square units)
- Cone & Pyramid volumes are 1/3 of corresponding prism

### Common Mistakes to Avoid:
- ❌ Mixing up radius and diameter
- ❌ Using degrees instead of radians for sector/arc
- ❌ Forgetting to square or cube in formulas
- ❌ Confusing slant height with vertical height

### Unit Conversions:
- 1 m² = 10,000 cm²
- 1 m³ = 1,000,000 cm³
- Always keep units consistent!

### Related Formulas:

```

Pyramid Volume = (1/3) × Base Area × Height
```
